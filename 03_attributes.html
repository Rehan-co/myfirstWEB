About SURINAME

Paramaribo

is the capital and largest city of Suriname, located on the banks of the Suriname River in the Paramaribo District. Paramaribo has a population of roughly 241,000 people (2012 census) , almost half of Suriname's population.The historic inner city of Paramaribo has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2002.

Wanica

is a district of Suriname located in the north of the country. Wanica's capital city is Lelydorp, the second-largest city in Suriname. Wanica has a population of 118,222 and an area of 443 km2.

Following neighboring Paramaribo, Wanica is the second most populated and urbanized district of Suriname. Two-thirds of the country's population live in these two districts.

Nickerie

is a predominantly rural district of Suriname located on the north-west coast. Nickerie's capital city is Nieuw-Nickerie. The district borders the Atlantic Ocean to the north,

Coronie to the east, Sipaliwini to the south and East Berbice-Corentyne in Guyana to the west.
